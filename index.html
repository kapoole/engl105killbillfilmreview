Chapter One

Introduction

Kill Bill Vol. I directed by Quentin Tarantino was released in 2003 starring Uma Thurman. T he pregnant and unnamed Bride (Uma Thurman) barely survives the massacre of her wedding rehearsal by five members of the Deadly Viper Assassination Squad and instead is left on death’s door in a coma for four years. She awakens from her coma to find herself no longer pregnant and with revenge on her mind. The Bride creates a kill list of the five people responsible for the massacre and sets out to serve some cold revenge. Tarantino used chapters in order to separate the story into smaller pieces; however, these pieces are delivered to the audience out of order. By developing the narrative in this manner, the ending is not meant to catch the audience off guard since the ending of the move is not the ending of the storyline. In this sense, the audience already knows what happens. How can a movie establish tension if the audience knows the result? Tarantino shifts the tension-building role from the plot and lets the music, violence, references to a variety of genres, jumps in the timeline take full rein. This shift changes the audience’s question from ‘What is going to happen?’ to ‘How is it going to happen?’. Kill Bill Vol. I is founded on a simplistic plot of revenge but enhances the storytelling in order to keep the audience engaged and excited rather than surprised.
